Breathing

Breathing plays a key-role in living mindfully: by focusing on the breath, we can calm both our minds and bodies. Studies show that breathing affects noradrenaline, a natural chemical in the brain often referred to as one of the “stress hormones”. When we produce too much it increases our heart rate and blood pressure, which is part of the body’s normal fight-or-flight response. Try instead to take full breaths: inhale deeply through your nose for 3 seconds, hold your breath for another 3 seconds, and exhale through your mouth for 3 seconds.

Progressive muscle relaxation

The key with this technique is to tense each muscle group progressively and hold for 5 seconds. Remember to exhale as you let your muscles relax for 10 to 20 seconds. Repeat 2 to 3 times per muscle group before you move on to the next muscle group. This exercise will help you lower your overall tension and stress levels, it also helpds reduce physical problems such as stomachaches and headaches, as well as improve your sleep.

Walking

Exercise increases oxygen and blood flow to all parts of the body, including the brain. Walking is just the perfect light exercise that helps you increase or maintain your overall health. Walking for 30 minutes a day on most days is perfect to get started!

Journaling

Journaling helps you remain present while keeping perspective in your everyday life. This practice can help you achieve goals, keep better track of your intentions and your progress. All this leads to increased self-confidence.

Body scan

When you mentally scan your body from head to toes you bring awareness to all the parts of your body: this helps you noticing any tensions or general discomfort, and while doing so if you focus on taking deep breaths a sensation of relief reaches your mind and helps sooth the discomfort.

Meditation

Meditation is an ancient practice that can produce a deep state of relaxation and a tranquil mind. During meditation, you focus your attention on how you're feeling rather than on the thoughts that tend to crowd our minds. These thought cause general stress and anxiety but by meditating we can learn to recenter ourselves with our bodies and minds.
